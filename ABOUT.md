# About Review Assistant

Review Assistant is a research workflow skill for turning Zotero paper collections into grounded literature-review outputs.

It works with local Zotero metadata and PDF attachments, then uses LLMs to extract structured findings, verify claims, synthesize reports, and identify follow-up literature. Its main goal is not just to summarize papers, but to keep review writing tied to source evidence through quote verification, citation checks, cached extraction, and explicit handling of missing PDFs.

The skill is useful for:

- auditing Zotero collections and PDF coverage;
- creating structured notes from batches of papers;
- checking whether review claims are supported by source papers;
- synthesizing a research question across a paper collection;
- generating review reports, narrative articles, summary tables, and diagrams;
- finding additional papers through Semantic Scholar and preparing Zotero imports.

Review Assistant is designed for iterative review work: inspect a collection, extract evidence, synthesize, verify, identify gaps, add papers, and rerun with cached intermediate results.
